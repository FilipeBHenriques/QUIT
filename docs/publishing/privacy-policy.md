# Privacy Policy for QUIT

Last updated: 2026-04-26

## Overview

QUIT is an Android app that helps users block selected apps and websites to reduce distraction and compulsive use.

QUIT is designed to work primarily on-device. Most blocking preferences, app state, and in-app stats are stored locally on the user's device.

QUIT does not require account creation.

## Information Processed by the App

To deliver its core blocking features, QUIT may process the following information on-device:

- blocked apps selected by the user
- blocked websites selected by the user
- app usage state needed to detect foreground apps
- browser URL or domain information needed to detect blocked websites in supported browsers
- app settings and timing preferences
- in-app stats and gameplay-related data generated by the app

## How Information Is Used

QUIT uses this information to:

- detect when a blocked app is opened
- detect when a blocked website is visited in a supported browser
- display a blocking screen
- remember blocked apps, blocked websites, and app settings
- show progress, usage, and in-app stats

## Local Storage

Preferences and app state are stored locally on the device using app storage.

## Advertising

QUIT uses Google AdMob to show ads. Google AdMob may collect and process data such as device identifiers, diagnostics, and advertising-related information according to Google's policies and privacy practices.

Google's Privacy Policy:

https://policies.google.com/privacy

## Permissions and Special Access

QUIT may request the following Android permissions or special access because they are required for core app features:

- Accessibility Service
- Usage Access
- Display Over Other Apps
- Battery Optimization Exemption
- Foreground Service

Accessibility Service is used to help detect supported browser website visits and app transitions that are necessary for blocking features selected by the user.

Usage Access is used to detect foreground app usage and support app blocking and time tracking.

Display Over Other Apps is used to show the blocking screen above blocked content.

## Data Sharing

QUIT does not sell personal information.

Aside from service providers such as Google AdMob, QUIT is not intended to share the user's blocked apps, blocked websites, or local app configuration with third parties.

## Data Retention

Because most app data is stored locally on the device, it is generally retained until the user removes it, clears app storage, or uninstalls the app.

## User Choices

Users can:

- change or remove blocked apps and websites in the app
- revoke permissions in Android settings
- clear app data through Android settings
- uninstall the app at any time

## Children's Privacy

QUIT is not specifically directed to children.
